The esteemed Punahele Competition is a time-honored tradition in the Aiu family, in which the sitting matriarch (herein referred to as "Grammy") of the family ranks her children and grandchildren in order from most to least favorite.


Degree of Grammy favoritism is determined by an individual's total Grammy points.


The family member with the most Grammy points is deemed the favorite and therefore crowned the Punahele.


Legend has it that having Punahele status grants the individual unspeakable fortune.


The family member with the fewest points is deemed the ugly redheaded step child and is at risk of familial ostracization.


Associating with the ugly redheaded step child is strongly discouraged and could also lead to ostracization.


Grammy points are awarded and deducted on a case-by-case basis.


The Grammy point value of each action is determined by precedent, and absent of precendent point values are require 2/3s "Ay" votes from committee of Kupuna.


Grammy reserves the right to veto and assign points as she sees fit without prior Kupuna committee approval.


It is estimated that a Grammy point is worth $0.0213 (12/2018 USD).
